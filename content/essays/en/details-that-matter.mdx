## The Common Thread

Every comparison above follows the same principle: **respect the user's perception**. Mathematical correctness and visual correctness are different things. A label that disappears might be "cleaner," but it fails the person using it. A button that looks the same whether it's active or not is technically consistent, but it confuses.

Details compound. One polished interaction is forgettable. A hundred of them make software feel _alive_. The difference between good and great is rarely a feature — it's the sum of a thousand small acts of care.
